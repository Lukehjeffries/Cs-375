This week I contributed to the development of the project requirements document. As a group, we worked together to define the core functionality of our course planner and outline what features the system should include. My role involved helping identify key requirements for the application, such as the ability for users to browse available courses, select courses to build a schedule, and manage potential scheduling conflicts. Working on this document helped us clarify the scope of the project and ensure that everyone on the team has a shared understanding of the system we are trying to build.

In addition to defining functional requirements, we also discussed the overall expectations for how the system should operate from a user perspective. This included thinking about usability, system limitations, and what features would realistically be achievable within the timeframe of the semester. Documenting these requirements early in the development process helps guide future design and implementation decisions.

Group collaboration was an important part of this process. Our team met to review ideas, provide feedback on each other’s contributions, and refine the document together. We discussed different approaches to the project and ensured that everyone’s input was considered when shaping the final requirements. Through these discussions, we were able to better organize the project goals and establish a clearer direction for upcoming development work.

Overall, creating the requirements document helped our team establish a structured plan for the project and improved communication within the group. It also provided a foundation that we can refer back to as we move forward into the design and implementation stages of the course planner.
